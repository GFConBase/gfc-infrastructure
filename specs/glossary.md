# Glossary

This glossary defines key terms used across the specifications
in this repository. Terms are defined for clarity and consistency.

---

## Core Terms

**GFC (German Foundation Coin)**  
The token and associated infrastructure described in this repository.

**Specification (Spec)**  
A document describing intended behavior, constraints, and boundaries
prior to implementation.

**On-chain**  
State, logic, or transactions recorded on a blockchain and independently verifiable.

**Off-chain**  
Processes or decisions not executed on-chain, but intended to be documented
in a predictable and reviewable way.

---

## Presale Terms

**Presale**  
A time-bound sale process intended to distribute tokens under predefined rules.

**Fixed Price**  
A constant token price defined by the presale design.
Frontend conversions may be used for display only.

**Instant Distribution**  
Token transfer to the buyer at the time of purchase (no later claim phase).

**Softcap**  
A minimum threshold that defines whether the presale is considered successful.

**Refund**  
Return of contributed funds to participants under predefined conditions
(e.g. softcap not reached by presale end).

**Withdrawal**  
Movement of collected presale funds out of the presale contract
under predefined rules (e.g. after softcap conditions are met).

**Unsold Tokens**  
Tokens allocated to the presale that are not purchased.
Their handling must follow predefined and documented rules.

---

## Governance Terms

**Governance**  
Decision-making processes and authority boundaries
related to infrastructure and fund flows.

**Constraint**  
A limitation that reduces discretionary control,
narrows possible actions, and improves predictability.

**Safeguard**  
A rule, process, or mechanism intended to prevent misuse,
reduce risk, or enforce accountability.

**Multisig (Multi-signature Wallet)**  
A wallet requiring multiple approvals to execute transactions.

---

## Status

This glossary may be expanded as specifications evolve.
Definitions are intended to remain stable unless explicitly revised.
